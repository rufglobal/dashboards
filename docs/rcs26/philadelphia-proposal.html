About the Romanian Community Summit

The Romanian Community Summit is the premier annual gathering of the Romanian-American community, organized by RUF under the auspices of the Embassy of Romania to the United States and the Consulate General of Romania in Chicago. RCS25 featured the Romanian Minister of Foreign Affairs, Minister of Education, the Ambassador of Romania to the US, five Ambassadors and Consuls General, Father Constantin Necula, and 45 youth Heritage Award recipients — representing communities from across the US, Canada, and Romania.

RCS26 expands to a 3-day, federated summit: partner organizations run their own thematic events within the summit framework. Morning sessions are open to the public (800–1,000/day at $15–$20); afternoon sessions serve 200 registered participants. The event streams live on GlobalRomania.

Why Philadelphia Church of God

We are coming to you not just because of your facilities — but because of what this partnership represents. Philadelphia Church of God has been a cornerstone of the Romanian community in Chicago for decades. Your congregation embodies the same values the Summit is built around: faith, family, unity, service, and love for Romania. Hosting RCS26 at Philadelphia would send a powerful signal to the entire diaspora that our church and our civic institutions stand together — one community, walking forward in the same direction. We believe this would be a historic and deeply meaningful occasion for your congregation and for every Romanian-American who attends.

We would be honored to meet with you and the council.

We are committed to making this a partnership that works well for everyone involved and look forward to building this milestone together.
